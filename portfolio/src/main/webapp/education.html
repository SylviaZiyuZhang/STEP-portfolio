    • My high school, Northeast Yucai School (东北育才学校/NEYC), is located in Shenyang, China. Built by Japanese colonizers about a century ago, it went through several significant adjustments and became a province flagship STEM school in recent decades. While the majority of the graduates of NEYC go through the Chinese college entrance exam, the school still provides us with a variety of elective courses and extracurricular opportunities.

    Here are a few random facts about my high school journey

    • I served as the president of the student body for two years.
    • I learned gas pistol and volleyball in my P.E. classes.
    • I learned to code in the competitive programming class of the school. As computer science wasn't a required class in the standard curriculum, I couldn't have been here without the help of my high school coach.
